Shisir Sapkota

I’m Shisir Sapkota — many people know me as Mama07.

I was born on March 11, 2004, in Melamchi, Sindhupalchok — a place where nature speaks in waterfalls, rivers carve their own paths, and mountains quietly teach you what strength looks like. Growing up there didn’t just shape my surroundings, it shaped my mindset. Life in a small village teaches you early that nothing comes easy — but everything is possible if you’re willing to work for it. As the eldest son of my family, responsibility was never something I learned later — it became part of me from a very young age. My father, Ramakrishna Sapkota, my mother, Sabita Sapkota, my brother Prashant Sapkota, and my grandmother Bhagawati Sapkota are the foundation of who I am. Their sacrifices, values, and support built the discipline and humility I carry with me every day.

My journey into engineering started early. In 2076, I chose the technical stream of Civil Engineering from class 9 at Shree Indreshwari Secondary School, Melamchi. For four years, I didn’t just study — I learned how to think, how to stay patient, and how to build something from the ground up. In 2079, I graduated as a Sub-Engineer, carrying both knowledge and the hunger to go further.

With more than six months of practical experience in the construction field, I began understanding what real engineering means — responsibility, precision, and the impact of every decision made on the ground. It taught me that engineering is not just about structures… it’s about trust.

Today, I am continuing that journey as an IOE student (since 2081) at Kathford International College of Engineering, Balkumari, Lalitpur. Step by step, I am working toward becoming a professional engineer — not just to build structures, but to build a meaningful life in my own country, Nepal.

But my story is not limited to engineering. On September 15, 2021, I started my YouTube channel, SPKVlog07 — completely on my own. No team, no shortcuts. Just a simple intention: to capture memories. Over time, that intention turned into something bigger. Vlogging became a way to express, to explore, and to document life as it is — raw, real, and evolving.I am someone who loves adventure, who finds peace in exploring new places, and who believes that memories are the most valuable things we can collect. Every video I create is not just content — it’s a piece of my journey, a moment I chose to remember.I consider myself a slow learner — but a consistent one. I take time, I make mistakes, but I never stop moving forward. I’m slightly introverted, I keep my circle small, but I value depth over numbers. I am soft-hearted by nature, but my mindset has been shaped by struggle — and that has made it strong.There have been phases in my life that tested me deeply. Times when things didn’t go as planned, when growth felt slow, and when silence became my companion. But those moments didn’t break me — they built me.

A man who never laughs the same again.

That line doesn’t represent weakness. It represents growth. It represents a phase where life teaches you seriousness, clarity, and purpose. People see me as hardworking, genuine, and loyal. I don’t try to prove myself loudly — I believe in working quietly and letting results speak over time.

My goal is clear: To grow as an engineer.To create meaningful content. To inspire people not through perfection, but through reality.

Because I believe —

You don’t have to be the fastest, you just have to keep going. You don’t need a big start to build a big future. And even the quietest dreamer can create something powerful. This is not a finished story. This is someone still building, still learning, still becoming.

And this… is just the beginning. 🚀
